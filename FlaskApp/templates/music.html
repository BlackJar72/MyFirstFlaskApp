Experiments in Music composition

Here is some music I've written while playing composer. Its it all synthesized, and was mixed and mastered using Audacity. Not that some of the music is not well written for the instruments its intended for; this is especially true of the clarinet, which I used frequently but knew nothing about how it was actually played. Thus came I up with music that sound good from a machine but would be very hard for a real musician to actually play. All music is copyright © Jared Blackburn, 2006-2008.

Pseduo-Clasical

The Mini-Symphony I

This is piece writen for small orchestra, and has two movements. Early versions added two more movements, though these really added nothing new. and later "Attainment" was placed as the final movement of three, but this also made no sense, as it was in a different style and for a larger and more modern orchestra. Early arrangements were longer through the use of repeats to stretch it out. I let the first and second movement stand on their own now.


The Canons I

This contains two canonical sections, with different time intervals between then lines of music, each of which could be a complete round on its own. The the longer of these is polyphonic and obvious, while the shorter is written to be heard as a series of cords. They have then been fit together so that the shorter sound like a kind of bridge or chorous between versus. Thus the name, this is not a canon, but the merging of two.

Attainment

My "masterpiece," at least in so far as this was the highest level of mastery I attained at music composition. This is arranged for a full modern orchestra.
